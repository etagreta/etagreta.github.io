\section*{\faIcon{user} Professional profile}

I am a mathematician specializing in category theory, type theory, and theoretical computer science, with a strong drive to apply foundational ideas to real-world AI systems. My interest sits at the intersection of categorical and probabilistic reasoning: I am passionate about working out how compositionality can guide inference, enabling systems that are both expressive and transparent.\vspace{.2cm}

I thrive in roles that require moving between abstraction and implementation, translating rigorous mathematical structure into something people can understand and build with. I am collaborative, interdisciplinary by nature, and most energized when working with people with different perspectives.

\section*{\faIcon{building} Relevant work experience}

\subsection*{MIRAI Srl \hfill Mar 2024 - present}
\textit{AI Research Scientist, Co-founder} \\
Co-founded a company assessing ethics issues in AI, where I contribute to the development of formal and probabilistic methods applied to real-world systems. My R\&D work includes designing and implementing frameworks for fairness assessment in ML classification, formal approaches to risk quantification, and end-to-end evaluation pipelines for AI systems deployed by public and private sector clients.

\subsection*{University of Pavia, AI Dept. \hfill Mar 2025 - present}
\textit{Adjunct professor in Logics for AI} \\
Lectured for the Logics course in the joint Bachelor’s program in Artificial Intelligence (UniPv--UniMi--UniMiB), with a focus on Bayesian networks and probabilistic graphical models for AI engineering students. Teaching at this level requires translating formal probabilistic reasoning into terms that engineering students can immediately apply, meaning I got to sharpen both my mathematics and my communication skills.

\subsection*{Segrate City Council \hfill Sep 2020 - present}
\textit{Majority chief whip} \\
Co-organized a civic movement and was elected to my city’s council on a civic list. Currently serving as chief whip of the largest council group and as vice-president of the council. Responsibilities include coordinating council activities on key policy issues, drafting and advancing legislative proposals, negotiating with institutional offices and stakeholders, and organizing public events to inform and actively engage citizens in shaping and implementing public policy.

\subsection*{University of Milan, Philosophy Dept. \hfill Mar 2023 - July 2025}
\textit{Postdoctoral researcher} \\
Conducted research in the BRIO (Bias, Risk, and Opacity in AI) project, advancing formal methods to assess AI fairness and risk. I co-developed open-source tools in partnership with private companies for real-world applications, such as to credit scoring. I benefitted from working within a multidisciplinary team, combining strong technical problem-solving with effective cross-sector communication.

\subsection*{University of Genoa, Mathematics Dept. \hfill Nov 2019 - Aug 2023}
\textit{Doctoral candidate}\\
Worked for a Ph.D. in Mathematics with specialization in logic, category theory, type theory, and theoretical computer science. I developed a strong foundation in rigorous problem-solving and abstract reasoning, with practical applications to formal system design, programming language theory, and algorithmic thinking.

During this time, I also taught a Linear Algebra (Geometry) course to first-year Electronic Engineering students. That strengthened my ability to explain complex technical concepts clearly, while reinforcing my expertise in linear algebra with an emphasis on its engineering applications in data modeling.

\section*{\faIcon{atom} Selected research}

Applied enriched category theory to develop syntax and semantics for a fuzzy type theory, targeting probabilistic programming and opinion dynamics. \\
\faIcon{angle-right}\, Presented as a poster at ACT 2023 and at the Logic Colloquium (Jun 2023).

Studied algebraic properties of parametric endofunctors, co/monads, and their algebras using fibered category theory, producing abstract structural results about their behaviour. \\
\faIcon{angle-right}\, Invited talks at HoTTEST (Mar 2024) and CATNIP (May 2024). Submitted for publication.
